We're now growing oyster mushrooms on coffee sacks!

Part of our ongoing quest to develop sustainable mushroom growing systems that are accesible and easy to learn.

We have developed a method using coffee sacks as a substrate for grey oyster mushrooms. There are many reasons why coffee sack fabric is perfect for this and we think we are on to something with this.

Jute / hessian fibres are coarse and loosely woven, which means they allow air exchange. The sack acts as both the container and the nutrient source, eliminiating the need for single use plastic bags. Coffee sacks are made from plant-based fibres, so they can break down over time and even be digested by the mycelium. While breathable, jute also holds moisture well - important for maintaining humidity. Easy to handle and adaptable - can roll into logs, stack as bricks or layer like lasagne. The nutrients are hard to access for many micro-organisms, fungi are able to digest coffee sacks easier than bacteria.
